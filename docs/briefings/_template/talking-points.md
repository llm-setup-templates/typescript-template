# Talking points — <event>

> Spoken delivery notes. One block per slide. These are the words you
> would actually say — not the slide text.

## Slide 1: Cover

(30 seconds) Introduce yourself, state the topic, state what you want
the audience to leave with.

> "I'm <name>. I'm here to <one-sentence purpose>. By the end of the
> next N minutes I want you to leave with three things: ..."

## Slide 2: The question

(60 seconds) Why this briefing exists. The context that made this
worth everyone's time.

## Slide 3: What we measured

(90 seconds) Walk through the method briefly, then land on the
headline number. Don't dwell on methodology if the audience isn't
engineering.

## Slide 4: What we found

(90 seconds) Name the one or two distinct problems. Keep the
engineering jargon on the slide; narrate in plain language.

## Slide 5: Decision

(60 seconds) Read the decision out loud. Make the rejected
alternatives explicit — "we considered X, but it would have cost Y."

## Slide 6: What's next

(60 seconds) Concrete sprint targets. If a sprint is the wrong unit
for your audience, translate to a calendar date.

## Slide 7: Honest limits

(60 seconds) The section where you gain trust by volunteering what
you can't do. Frame positively: "we designed for this" not "we
failed at this."

> "Tier 1 coverage in Korean is zero. That's not a failure — it's
> a data gap. We designed our cascade specifically so that gaps show
> as `score = NULL` rather than guesses. Sprint 2 closes the gap with
> the adapter pattern."

## Slide 8: Close / Q&A

(30 seconds) Repeat the three takeaways from slide 1. Open Q&A.
